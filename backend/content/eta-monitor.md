Some torrents appear healthy but have an extremely high ETA due to a sparse swarm or very few active seeds. They'll sit in your queue for weeks making minimal real progress while qBittorrent reports them as "downloading." This module identifies torrents with a persistently bad ETA and removes them so Sonarr and Radarr can search for a better-seeded source.

To avoid false positives, several thresholds work together. A torrent must reach a minimum age before ETA checking begins, giving new downloads time to find peers. Once a bad ETA is detected a grace period starts — the torrent is only removed if the bad ETA is **sustained** and the speed stays below the minimum threshold for the full grace period. This means a torrent that briefly looks slow but recovers is left alone. Downloads near completion are protected by the ignore-above-progress threshold, since a nearly-finished torrent with a bad ETA is almost always worth waiting out.
